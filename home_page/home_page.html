"Forests: The Earth's Lungs and Biodiversity Hotspots"

        Forests act as the Earth's lungs by absorbing carbon dioxide and releasing oxygen, playing a crucial role in regulating the global climate. They are also vital biodiversity hotspots, hosting more than 80% of the world’s terrestrial species. The complex ecosystems within forests provide habitat and sustenance for countless plants, animals, and microorganisms, contributing to the overall health and stability of the environment. Protecting these rich ecosystems is essential for sustaining life on Earth and combating climate change

        Forests are vital for maintaining atmospheric balance, producing oxygen, and sequestering carbon. They are also rich in biodiversity, supporting a vast array of species and ecosystems. These ecosystems regulate climate, influence weather patterns, and provide critical resources. Safeguarding forests is essential for preserving global health, ecological diversity, and ensuring sustainable development.

Forest For Us

        The Forestry Division spearheads efforts to promote sustainable forest management, balancing economic, social, and environmental interests across forested landscapes. It engages with member countries, partners, civil society, and the private sector to advocate for effective policies and enhance the role of forests in mitigating climate change and aiding adaptation efforts.

        Additionally, the Division is responsible for gathering, analyzing, and disseminating information on global forest resources, including the production, trade, and consumption of both wood and non-wood forest products. The Committee on Forestry, along with six regional forestry commissions, provides guidance to us in our technical forestry initiatives.

Events

International Day of Forests (March 21)

India observes the International Day of Forests, established by the United Nations, on March 21 each year. The day is marked with various activities across the country, including workshops, seminars, and tree planting drives. The focus is on educating the public about the critical role of forests in sustaining biodiversity, combating climate change, and supporting livelihoods.

Wildlife Week (October 1-7)

Although primarily focused on wildlife, Wildlife Week in India also emphasizes the role of forests in supporting biodiversity. Celebrated every year from October 1 to October 7, the week involves educational programs, wildlife conservation activities, and public awareness campaigns about the importance of forests in providing habitat for wildlife and maintaining ecological balance.

Van Mahotsav (Tree Plantation Day)

Van Mahotsav, or Tree Plantation Day, is celebrated annually from July 1 to July 7 in India. Initiated in 1950 by then Union Minister for Agriculture, K. M. Munshi, this week-long event aims to promote tree planting and raise awareness about the importance of forests. Schools, communities, and government agencies across the country participate by planting trees, organizing educational activities, and spreading messages about forest conservation.
